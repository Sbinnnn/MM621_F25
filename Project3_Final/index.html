MM621:Subin Choi (Floating Bowls Meditation)

A small sound playground created by floating bowls and soft collision sounds on the water

Concept

When the user clicks anywhere on the screen, a bowl appears on the water. The bowls float and bounce like light balls, and they create different sounds when they gently hit each other. With water-like shadows and a background image, the interface expresses the idea of “small sound objects drifting on the water.”

1. Instructions

  1. Click anywhere on the screen to release a bowl.
  2. Each bowl drifts slowly in a random direction.
  3. When two bowls collide, they produce a soft bell-like sound.
  4. Enjoy the shifting movement and accidental harmony as the bowls interact.

2. Visual Concept

  • Background Image: A full-screen image that looks like still water or a tank is used as the background.
  • Bowl Images: Six different bowl images are randomly selected to create a different visual composition each time.
  • Blue Ellipse Shadow: A soft blue ellipse is drawn under each bowl to give the feeling that it is slightly floating on the water (not affecting physics).

3. Sound Concept

  • Prepare eight different sound clips.
  • When a collision happens between bowls, one sound is randomly chosen from the array and played.
  • If a sound is already playing, it does not overlap too much, preventing excessive noise.

4. Where the Inspiration Came From

Floating Bowls began with a simple question: “Can we create a moment of pause and calm even in a digital space?” Instead of a complex UI or task-oriented interaction, I believed that slow movement and soft sound alone can bring the user into a meditative state.

  • The slow and gentle movement of objects floating on water
  • The light rhythm created when they softly touch each other
  • The small sense of chance that comes from unexpected patterns

Together, these elements guide the user toward an interaction that feels more like observing than controlling. I see this as an experimental interface that can offer small moments of rest and sensory focus within a digital experience.
